About Me

I am a Philosophy MA and Computer Engineering BSc graduate from Bilkent University. My passion lies in interdisciplinary studies in the sciences and arts, which I hope is reflected in my career.

My masters research interest was focused on the Philosophy of Artificial Intelligence. In my thesis, "A Re-examination of Computational Creativity through Non-Human Animals," I worked on the theory of computational creativity by drawing observations from creativity in non-human animals. During my undergraduate years, I also had many exciting opportunities. I worked for Walt Disney World as part of the Disney Cultural Exchange Program. I worked for Oracle Corporation as a summer intern. I also had the honor of presenting my paper "A Discourse on Ergodic Life via Mark. Z. Since I graduated, I have been working as a self-employed web and graphic designer, as I pursue job opportunities where my unique and diverse background can be of service. I am also working toward a dream of telling stories through animation and video games by working as a programmer and artist in a project with friends.
